About

Cirrhosis, also known as hepatic cirrhosis or end-stage liver disease , is a long-term condition that occurs when healthy liver cells are replaced by scar tissue, preventing the liver from functioning normally. The scar tissue is known as fibrosis .Cirrhosis is often caused by hepatitis and other viruses, as well as alcohol abuse.
Other causes include: Nonalcoholic fatty liver disease, Chronic hepatitis C, and Chronic hepatitis B.
